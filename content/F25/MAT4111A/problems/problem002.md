# Problem: midpoints between lattice points

<p>A lattice point in the plane is a point \((x,y)\) such that both \(x\) and \(y\) are integers. Find the smallest number \(n\) such that given \(n\) lattice points in the plane, there exist two whose midpoint is also a lattice point.</p>

(From <a href="https://mast.queensu.ca/~mikeroth/putnam/handouts/solpigeon.pdf">Putnam Pigeonhole Principle</a> by Mike Roth)
